Write lyrics or verse to improve your language skills

Any literature has form - either what is more important goes first, or it's a story told chronologically. Unlike prose, lyrics have rhythm and melody, which are closer to natural speech than the strict meter of poetry. Both can have rhymes and alliterations, which might include only the vowel or consonant of a syllable. It is more important that the word containing them is a keyword, than the rhyme's position within a line.
